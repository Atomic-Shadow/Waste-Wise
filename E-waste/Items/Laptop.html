Why Proper Disposal Matters

Environmental Damage

Toxic substances can leach into soil and water, polluting the environment. Burning e-waste releases dangerous gases that harm the atmosphere.

Resource Waste

Laptops contain valuable metals like gold, silver, copper, and rare earths. Improper disposal leads to loss of non-renewable resources.

Health Risks

Exposure to leaked chemicals like lead, cadmium, and lithium can cause: Nerve and brain damage, Skin and respiratory irritation, Organ failure from long-term exposure.

How to Dispose

Electronics recycling
  1. Backup important data.
  2. Wipe the hard drive (factory reset or secure erase).
  3. Remove battery (if possible).
  4. Do not throw in regular trash.
  5. Drop off at an e-waste recycling center.
Manufacturer take-back programs

Alternative disposal method for your convenience.

Where to Dispose of laptops
  1. Authorized e-waste recycling centers.
  2. Manufacturer take-back programs (e.g., HP, Dell, Apple).
  3. Electronics retailers (Best Buy, Croma, Reliance Digital).
  4. Municipal collection drives.
  5. NGOs or schools (for reuse or refurbishing).

Reuse and Upcycling Options

Reuse screen with Raspberry Pi as a monitor.

Salvage hard drive for external storage.

Reuse keyboard as a USB keyboard.

Use cooling fans in electronics cooling systems.

Turn the body into a tech-themed notebook cover.
