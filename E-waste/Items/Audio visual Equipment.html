Why Proper Disposal Matters

Environmental Damage

Toxic metals can leach into soil and water, contaminating ecosystems. Burning AV equipment releases harmful gases and pollutants. Improper dumping causes long-term environmental degradation.

Resource Waste

AV equipment contains valuable metals like copper, gold, silver, and rare earth elements. Proper recycling recovers these materials, conserving natural resources. Reduces the need for mining and energy consumption.

Health Risks

Exposure to heavy metals causes neurological, respiratory, and kidney problems. Broken equipment can cause physical injury from sharp components. Toxic fumes from burning plastics or electronics are harmful to humans.

How and Where to Dispose

Electronics recycling

Do not discard in regular trash bins. Dispose through:

  • E-waste recycling centers.
  • Manufacturer or retailer take-back programs.
  • Local hazardous waste collection events.
  • Remove batteries separately for proper disposal.
  • Package equipment carefully to avoid damage during transport.

Additional information
  • Many countries have regulations mandating safe e-waste disposal.
  • Recycling reduces landfill waste and greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Donation of working AV equipment extends its life and reduces waste.
  • Public education campaigns improve awareness of e-waste hazards.
  • Workers handling e-waste should use protective gear.
  • Some components can be refurbished or reused, promoting circular economy.
