Why Proper Disposal Matters

Environmental Damage

Toxic materials can leak into soil and water, harming ecosystems. Burning tablets releases harmful gases that pollute the air.

Resource Waste

Tablets contain valuable resources like gold, silver, cobalt, and rare earth metals. Improper disposal wastes these non-renewable materials.

Health Risks

Toxic substances can cause: Brain and nerve damage (from lead and mercury), Skin and lung irritation (from lithium leaks), Organ damage (from cadmium and arsenic) People handling e-waste without safety gear are especially at risk.

How to Dispose

Electronics recycling
  1. Back up and erase data (factory reset)
  2. Remove accessories(case, SD card, SIM)
  3. Do not throw in the trash
  4. Drop off at an authorized recycling point
Manufacturer take-back programs

Alternative disposal method for your convenience.

Where to Dispose of Tablets
  1. E-waste recycling centers
  2. Brand take-back programs (Apple, Samsung, Lenovo, etc.)
  3. Electronics stores (Best Buy, Croma, Reliance Digital)
  4. Municipal e-waste drives
  5. NGOs that accept old electronics for reuse or parts

Reuse and Upcycling Options
