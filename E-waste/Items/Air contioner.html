Why Proper Disposal Matters

Environmental Damage

Refrigerant leaks contribute to ozone layer depletion and are powerful greenhouse gases causing climate change. Toxic chemicals can leach into soil and water, harming ecosystems and wildlife. Burning or improper dismantling releases hazardous gases and pollutants. Plastics and metals add to landfill burden and contribute to pollution.

Resource Waste

Air conditioners contain valuable metals like copper, aluminum, and steel. Recycling reduces demand for mining and lowers carbon footprint. Proper disposal allows recovery of refrigerants and other reusable materials.

Health Risks

Exposure to refrigerants and heavy metals can cause respiratory issues, skin irritation, and neurological damage. Leaking refrigerants are flammable and toxic, posing fire and health hazards. Workers dismantling AC units without proper protection face increased health risks.

How to Dispose

Electronics recycling
  • Have certified professionals safely recover refrigerants before disposal.
  • Use authorized e-waste or appliance recycling centers.
  • Participate in manufacturer or retailer take-back programs.
  • Avoid illegal dumping or throwing AC units in regular trash.
  • Ensure proper dismantling of hazardous parts by trained personnel.
Manufacturer take-back programs

Alternative disposal method for your convenience.

Where to Dispose
  1. Certified appliance and e-waste recycling facilities specializing in HVAC units.
  2. Brand or retailer take-back and exchange programs (e.g., LG, Samsung, Whirlpool).
  3. Local municipal hazardous waste collection centers or events.
  4. NGOs or refurbishing centers accepting large appliances.

Reuse and Upcycling Options

Reclaim and reuse refrigerants (like R-32 or R-410A) in other AC units or cooling systems.

Reuse copper coils and wires in electrical or plumbing applications.

Repurpose the compressor and fan motor for DIY cooling or ventilation projects.

Recycle metal casing and parts for use in manufacturing or scrap metal markets.

Reuse plastic panels and components in making furniture, storage items, or crafts.
